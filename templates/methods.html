For every query arabidopsis gene, we found a 40 gene window centered on it, and check if we can find syntenic region in the target genome. We used LASTZ (default parameters) to define anchors, and require the syntenic region to have at least 4 collinear anchors (out of 40 possible anchors). Nearest anchors on both sides of genes are identified, in order to define a "tight" syntenic location.

Each query gene is then categorized, based on the flank anchors and more sensitive search on the tight interval.

  • Gene match in the interval - syntelog (S) or no syntelog (F)
  • Have both flankers (F) or one flanker (G)

For genes labeled as F, further validate.

  • BLAST matches (e.g. to non-coding sequences) in the interval (FB)
  • Assembly gaps (N's) in the interval (FN)
